My research focuses on developing explainable AI for time-series data in healthcare to predict heart attacks and associated complications. I also develop AI to predict the spread of infectious diseases globally (like COVID-19), think about the interplay of the law and the need for explainability of AI, and just like to work on using AI safely to improve people's lives and health.

I've been fortunate to have had a global education, completing my BSc in Electrical Engineering at NYU Abu Dhabi summa cum laude. I studied abroad and worked on cool projects during my undergrad in Oman, India, the UK, and the United States including excavation sites with National Geographic explorers.

I won a Rhodes Scholarship as the first Bosnian in history and then moved to Oxford to pursue my MSc in Computer Science and my PhD.

I'm passionate about the transformative power of technology and have worked in the disability space in the UAE and the US, have been on the Working Group for Trustworthy AI at Mozilla Foundation, and have co-founded a non-profit Oxford Diplomatic Society to leverage opportunities between the strategic diplomacy and technology spaces.

These questions are currently on my mind (pls reach out if they resonate and you want to chat!):

  • what makes an AI model explainable? what does 'explainable' even mean? to whom? can we verify that?
  • how do we safely develop AI to predict tricky diseases?
  • what are new representation learning approaches to longitudinal health data (I looked at sequential models, dynamic graphs, and some LLMs so far)?

research

My research approach is grounded in information theory and human-centered computing, but also draws on cognitive science and design studies. I directly work with both models and people, aiming to evaluate and enhance their interactions by developing and testing frameworks, as well as conducting experiments and user studies.
